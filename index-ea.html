Introduction

Emergence of 1920’s New Culture Movement in Taiwan

In the 1920s’ Taiwan under the rule of Japanese colonial government, those intellectuals who had accepted new knowledge, insisted on local consciousness and promoted cultural development, fighting for democracy and human rights through social movements. Among them, Taiwan Cultural Association established on October 17, 1921 was the earliest organization, declaring their aim "to encourage the development of Taiwanese culture". The series of actions taken by the pioneering intellectuals to upgrade public culture included publishing cultural bulletins, setting up free private schools, organizing cultural lectures, opening cultural bookstores, offering various types of knowledge workshops and summer schools, and so on. They not only began the enlightening movement in the 1920s’ Taiwan, but also became the pioneers to integrate "local culture" with "world’s civilization" in 20th century’s Taiwan. Many new attempts and activities were first made in the affluent Dadaocheng, and then spread across the whole island, making a great contribution to carrying out the New Culture Movement in Taiwan.

  1. Doctor Chiang Wei-shui

A forerunner of Taiwan’s cultural, political and social movements in 1920s, and also the helmsman fighting against Japanese colonial rule of Taiwan, Doctor Chiang Wei-shui, by combining the then Taiwanese elite, founded in 1921 the "Taiwan Cultural Association". In 1923, he set up "The Taiwan Minpao", a first newspaper by Taiwanese. He established the first political party in Taiwan, the "Taiwan People's Party" In 1927, and founded next year Taiwan's first laborer-farmer union, the "Taiwan Workers’ League". Doctor Chiang strove for the full realization of Taiwanese people's political freedom, economic emancipation and social equality. Through his life, despite being imprisoned more than dozen times, he devoted himself to the faith of bringing Taiwan to the state of utmost liberty, utmost equality and utmost civilization. On August 5, 1931, at the age of 40 years young, he died of typhoid fever, and was then respected as the "Savior of Taiwanese People".
